## The idea

First-year students often meet lab equipment for the first time under pressure,
and the resources available are usually flat, **2D** walkthroughs. I'd like to
build a **3D / VR** environment where they can rehearse using real equipment
hands-on before they ever set foot in the lab.

## Why it could matter

The point isn't just to show the ideal procedure — it's to capture the **small,
human mistakes** that actually happen, so students learn what to watch for:

- forgetting to put the lid back on before closing a centrifuge,
- not balancing the tubes by weight,
- and similar slips that an experienced demonstrator would catch.

Getting these wrong in a simulation is free; getting them wrong on real equipment
is not.

## Scope

The **minimum viable product** is a single, well-modelled piece of equipment — a
**centrifuge** — done properly, including those failure cases. If that proves
valuable, later stages could grow toward more advanced techniques for second- and
third-year (or higher) students, such as using a spectrophotometer or running a
western blot.
